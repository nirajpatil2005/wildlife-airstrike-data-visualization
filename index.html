WILDLIFE AIR strikes

Information

The project aims to analyze and mitigate the risks posed by wildlife strikes to military, commercial, and civil aircraft. Wildlife strikes occur when birds or other animals collide with aircraft during flight or takeoff/landing, potentially causing damage to the aircraft and endangering passengers and crew.

About Dataset

This dataset comprises a comprehensive collection of airplane-wildlife strike incidents involving military, commercial or civil aircraft. The incidents were sourced from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) Wildlife Strike Database. The dataset covers a range of information related to these incidents, including details about the aircraft involved, wildlife species, geographical locations and various factors contributing to the occurrences.

About terms

The dataset and data visualization utilize various terms and abbreviations.
Attached is a glossary of the terms and their corresponding shorthand notations
Key Features:
INCIDENT_DATE: Date of the incident | AIRPORT: Airport involved in the incident | AIRCRAFT: Aircraft information
SPECIES: Wildlife species involved in the strike | LOCATION: Geographical location of the incident
PHASE_OF_FLIGHT: Phase of the flight during which the incident occurred | DAMAGE_LEVEL: Level of damage resulting from the incident.

About Tools

Utilizing Power BI as our sole data analysis and visualization tool, we harnessed its extensive capabilities to conduct an in-depth examination of the dataset. Power BI’s advanced data processing and analytics features allowed us to identify key trends and patterns, facilitating a nuanced understanding of the data. The tool’s intuitive interface and rich visualization options enabled us to construct interactive dashboards that effectively communicated our findings. The decision to exclusively use Power BI was strategic, ensuring a streamlined workflow and a cohesive analytical narrative throughout our mini project.
